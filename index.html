About Me

What sets me apart from everyone else at my level is my unique life experiences. I served in the U.S. Army and then went on to become an Emergency Room Nurse. The skills I learned during both of those careers have shaped me into the person I am today; a dedicated, hard-working team player who always gets the job done. There is no quitting in either of those careers and there is no quitting in software development. There is a job to do and I am here to do it.

One of the main draws for me to software development is my love of life-long learning. In order to succeed, software needs to be relevant, fast, functional and serve the end user needs. This includes developing secure software and staying up to date on the latest security vulnerabilities. I subscribe to news releases from CISA and regularly visit the OWASP website to stay abreast of vulnerabilities so I can better develop applications with a focus on security.

In my spare time I enjoy trail running, indie game development and taking care of our farm animals. I just need 6 more hours each day to do everything I want.

I am confident that my unique combination of hard and soft skills is not only rare to find in anyone else, but it also extremely valuable to have as a member of your team. I am focused, driven and determined to stay abreast of current events and changing technologies. My day does not end at the end of regular business hours.
